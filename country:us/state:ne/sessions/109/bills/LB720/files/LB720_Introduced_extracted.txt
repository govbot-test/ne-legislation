2026 2026
1 Section 1. Section 38-1001, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
2 2024, is amended to read:
3 38-1001 Sections 38-1001 to 38-10,172 and section 2 of this act
4 shall be known and may be cited as the Cosmetology, Electrology,
5 Esthetics, Nail Technology, and Body Art Practice Act.
6 Sec. 2. (1) The department shall create and issue a temporary,
7 nonrenewable event license for an individual who is attending a body art
8 event which shall allow the individual to practice for no more than seven
9 consecutive days. An individual holding an event license under this
10 section shall only practice within a licensed body art facility.
11 (2) An individual applying for an event license described in
12 subsection (1) of this section shall hold a valid, unrestricted license
13 or permit showing the individual is active in another state and shall
14 submit the following documents to the department:
15 (a) A completed application form;
16 (b) A copy of a government-issued photographic identification
17 showing the individual's proof of age;
18 (c) A copy of the individual's body art license; and
19 (d) A sponsorship letter from a licensed body art facility or body
20 art event organizer.
21 (3) The event license shall include the (a) individual's name, (b)
22 approved location or event, and (c) expiration date.
23 (4) The department may charge a fee, not to exceed fifty dollars,
24 for issuance of the event license. The department shall remit the fee to
25 the State Treasurer for credit to the Professional and Occupational Cash
26 Credentialing Fund.
27 (5) The department shall be authorized to inspect a body art
28 facility to assess compliance with this section. A violation of this
29 section is subject to the same penalties as for an individual or a
30 facility holding a permanent license to practice body art under the
31 Uniform Credentialing Act.
