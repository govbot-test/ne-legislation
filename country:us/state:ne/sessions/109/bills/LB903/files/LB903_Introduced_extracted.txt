1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to public assistance; to amend section
2 68-1212, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement, 2024; to provide
3 for a referral for home visitation services for certain families by
4 case managers as prescribed; and to repeal the original section.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

LB903 LB903
2026 2026
1 Section 1. Section 68-1212, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
2 2024, is amended to read:
3 68-1212 For all cases in which a court has awarded a juvenile to the
4 care of the Department of Health and Human Services according to
5 subsection (1) of section 43-285 and for any noncourt and voluntary
6 cases, the case manager shall be an employee of the department. Such case
7 manager shall be responsible for and shall directly oversee: Case
8 planning; service authorization; investigation of compliance; monitoring
9 and evaluation of the care and services provided to children and
10 families; and decisionmaking regarding the determination of visitation
11 and the care, placement, medical services, psychiatric services,
12 training, and expenditures on behalf of each juvenile under subsection
13 (1) of section 43-285. Such case manager shall be responsible for
14 decisionmaking and direct preparation regarding the proposed plan for the
15 care, placement, services, and permanency of the juvenile filed with the
16 court required under subsection (2) of section 43-285. The health and
17 safety of the juvenile shall be the paramount concern in the proposed
18 plan. When appropriate, the case manager may refer families with a child,
19 two years of age or younger, for evidence-based home visitation services
20 as provided in the Family Home Visitation Act.
21 Sec. 2. Original section 68-1212, Revised Statutes Cumulative
22 Supplement, 2024, is repealed.
