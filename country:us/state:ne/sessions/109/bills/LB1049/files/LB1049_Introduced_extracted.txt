1 Section 1. (1) For purposes of this section, regulatory action
2 means the adoption, promulgation, amendment, or withdrawal of a rule or
3 regulation as defined in section 84-901.
4 (2) Along with a fiscal note, the Legislative Fiscal Analyst shall
5 prepare and submit an economic impact report to the appropriate standing
6 committee of the Legislature, for any legislative bill that would
7 require, as the result of being signed into law, regulatory action by an
8 agency as defined in section 84-901.
9 (3) The economic impact report shall include a cost benefit analysis
10 including:
11 (a) The estimated primary or direct benefits;
12 (b) The estimated cost savings or financial benefits to society;
13 (c) The estimated number of businesses or other entities to which
14 the regulatory action will apply;
15 (d) The identity of any localities and types of businesses or other
16 entities that will be particularly affected by the regulatory action;
17 (e) The estimated compliance costs for regulated entities, including
18 costs for affected businesses, localities, or entities;
19 (f) The estimated impact on the use and value of private property,
20 including additional costs related to the development of real estate for
21 commercial or residential purposes;
22 (g) The estimated effect on state expenditures, including estimated
23 administrative expenses;
24 (h) The estimated secondary or indirect costs; and
25 (i) Key assumptions and sources of uncertainty.
26 (4) State agencies shall cooperate with the Legislative Fiscal
27 Analyst by providing any information relevant to the economic impact
28 report.
