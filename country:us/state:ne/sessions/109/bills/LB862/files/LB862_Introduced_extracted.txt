1 Section 1. Section 79-2607, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 79-2607 (1) For purposes of this section:
4 (a) Evidence-based reading instruction means instruction in reading
5 that is in alignment with scientifically based reading research and does
6 not include the three-cueing system model of reading instruction; and
7 (b) Three-cueing system model of reading instruction is an approach
8 to foundational skills instruction that involves the use of three
9 different types of instructional cues which include semantic, syntactic,
10 and graphophonic.
11 (2)(a) The State Department of Education shall develop and implement
12 a professional learning system to help provide sustained professional
13 learning and training regarding evidence-based reading instruction for
14 teachers who teach children from four years of age through third grade at
15 an approved or accredited school and teachers employed by an early
16 childhood education program approved by the State Board of Education. The
17 professional learning system shall include information and tips for
18 teachers related to helping children and families work with local family
19 literacy centers to strengthen home and family literacy programs and
20 better instruct children in reading.
21 (b) Approved or accredited elementary schools and early childhood
22 education programs approved by the State Board of Education shall ensure
23 that teachers who teach children from four years of age through third
24 grade are aware of the professional learning system and are adequately
25 trained regarding evidence-based reading instruction to effectively
26 instruct students in reading.
27 (3) The State Department of Education shall work with educational
28 service units to provide regional coaches to approved or accredited
29 elementary schools to provide assistance and job-embedded training
30 relating to evidence-based reading instruction to teachers who teach
31 students in kindergarten through third grade.
