1 Section 1. Section 83-182, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 83-182 (1) The director shall establish appropriate programs for
4 each facility designed as far as practical to prepare and assist each
5 person committed to the department to assume his or her responsibilities
6 as a useful citizen. In developing such programs, the director shall seek
7 to make available to each person capable of benefiting therefrom academic
8 or vocational training, participation in productive work, religious and
9 recreational activities, and such therapeutic measures as are
10 practicable. No person shall be ordered or compelled to participate in
11 religious activities.
12 (2)(a) The director shall offer and make available educational
13 services and educational programming to any person committed to the
14 department who is under twenty-one years of age and who has not
15 previously earned a high school diploma or diploma of high school
16 equivalency. The director shall ensure special education services are
17 provided as required by state and federal law. Nothing in this subsection
18 requires participation by any person committed to the department in
19 educational services or educational programming except to the extent
20 necessary to provide services required by law.
21 (b) Such educational services shall be substantially similar to the
22 minimum standards as determined by the State Department of Education,
23 including a minimum curriculum that includes language arts, social
24 science, science, and mathematics, and may include other subjects
25 including, but not limited to, vocational education, computer education,
26 arts, and physical fitness. Such programs may be offered by the
27 department or through partnerships with educational institutions.
28 (3) The State Board of Education may adopt and promulgate rules and
29 regulations to carry out this section and to ensure the educational
30 services and educational programming meet any applicable educational
31 requirements.
