1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to the Nebraska Probate Code; to amend

7 Sec. 2. Section 30-2619, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
8 amended to read:
9 30-2619 (a) The person alleged to be incapacitated or any person
10 interested in his or her welfare may petition for a finding of incapacity
11 and appointment of a guardian or a standby guardian. The petition shall
12 be verified and shall contain specific allegations with regard to each of
13 the areas as provided under section 30-2619.01 in which the petitioner
14 claims that the person alleged to be incapacitated lacks sufficient
15 understanding to make or communicate responsible decisions concerning his
16 or her own person. An interested person may file a motion to make more
17 definite and certain requesting a specific description of the functional
18 limitations and physical and mental condition of the person alleged to be
19 incapacitated with the specific reasons prompting the request for
20 guardianship.
21 (b) Upon the filing of a petition, the court shall set a date for
22 hearing on the issues of incapacity and unless the person alleged to be
23 incapacitated has retained counsel of his or her own choice or has
24 otherwise indicated a desire for an attorney of his or her own choice,
25 the court may appoint an attorney to represent him or her in the
26 proceeding. The court may appoint a guardian ad litem as provided in
27 sections 30-4201 to 30-4210 to advocate for the best interests of the
28 person alleged to be incapacitated.
29 (c) The person alleged to be incapacitated may be examined by a
30 physician appointed by the court. The physician shall submit his or her
31 report in writing to the court and may be interviewed by a visitor, if so

26 provided by order of the county court.
27 (f) The Public Guardian shall not be appointed as a standby
28 guardian.
