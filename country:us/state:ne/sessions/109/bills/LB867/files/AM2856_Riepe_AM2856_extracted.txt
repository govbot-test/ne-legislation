2 Sec. 20. Section 71-1908, Revised Statutes Supplement, 2025, is
3 amended to read:
4 71-1908 (1) Sections 71-1908 to 71-1923.03 and section 21 of this
5 act shall be known and may be cited as the Child Care Licensing Act.
6 (2) The Legislature finds that there is a present and growing need
7 for quality child care programs and facilities. There is a need to
8 establish and maintain licensure of persons providing such programs to
9 ensure that such persons are competent and are using safe and adequate
10 facilities. The Legislature further finds and declares that the
11 development and supervision of programs are a matter of statewide concern
12 and should be dealt with uniformly on the state and local levels. There
13 is a need for cooperation among the various state and local agencies
14 which impose standards on licensees, and there should be one agency which
15 coordinates the enforcement of such standards and informs the Legislature
16 about cooperation among the various agencies.
17 Sec. 21. (1) The department shall establish a youth afterschool
18 eligibility letter for individuals who are sixteen or seventeen years of
19 age and who are seeking employment in school-age child care programs or
20 temporary nonresidential child care programs. For purposes of this
21 section, temporary nonresidential child care program means a program in
22 which child care is provided (a) for no more than twelve consecutive
23 hours, (b) not on a continuous or regularly scheduled basis, and (c) in a
24 location not ordinarily used as a licensed child care center.
25 (2)(a) To obtain a youth afterschool eligibility letter, an
26 applicant shall:
