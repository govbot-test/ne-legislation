1 Section 1. (1) Except as provided in this section, a person shall
2 not restrain a juvenile using shackling.
3 (2) A person who is authorized by law to restrain a juvenile may use
4 shackling to restrain such juvenile only if:
5 (a) The juvenile presents a substantial risk of inflicting physical
6 harm on himself or herself or others as evidenced by recent behavior;
7 (b) No less restrictive alternative will prevent such risk of harm;
8 and
9 (c) The use of shackling is limited to the duration strictly
10 necessary to prevent the risk of such harm.
11 (3) This section does not apply to the use of restraints under
12 section 43-251.03.
13 (4) For purposes of this section:
14 (a) Shackling means the use of any mechanical restraint, including
15 handcuffs, leg irons, waist chains, belly chains, ankle restraints, a
16 restraint chair, or any combination of such devices, that restricts the
17 movement of a juvenile's arms, hands, legs, waist, or torso; and
18 (b) Shackling does not include the brief, temporary use of handcuffs
19 solely to address an immediate and articulable safety risk if such
20 handcuffs are removed as soon as such risk has passed.
21 Sec. 2. Section 43-2,129, Revised Statutes Supplement, 2025, is
22 amended to read:
23 43-2,129 Sections 43-245 to 43-2,129 and section 1 of this act shall
24 be known and may be cited as the Nebraska Juvenile Code.
