1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to child welfare; to amend section 71-1904,
2 Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, and section 71-1924, Revised
3 Statutes Cumulative Supplement, 2024; to provide duties for the
4 Department of Health and Human Services relating to certain
5 curriculum and training materials; to require suicide awareness and
6 prevention training for child welfare workers, for certain
7 licensure, and for employees of child-placing agencies as
8 prescribed; to harmonize provisions; to provide an operative date;
9 and to repeal the original sections.
10 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

1 Section 1. For purposes of providing training for individuals
2 involved in the child welfare system, the Department of Health and Human
3 Services shall develop a baseline of curriculum and training materials
4 related to suicide awareness and prevention in consultation with
5 statewide and national organizations and professionals with expertise in
6 behavioral and mental health and suicide prevention, including, but not
7 limited to, the Nebraska State Suicide Prevention Coalition and the
8 Behavioral Health Education Center of Nebraska. All such suicide
9 awareness and prevention training shall incorporate evidence-based
10 practices and may include, but not be limited to, (1) identification of
11 early warning signs and symptoms of behavioral and mental health issues
12 for youth in the child welfare system and (2) appropriate and effective
13 responses for behavioral and mental health issues and trauma-informed
14 care.
15 Sec. 2. All staff involved in the child welfare system of the
16 Department of Health and Human Services that provide direct services to
17 children or families or interact directly with children or families, and
18 any other appropriate personnel as determined by the Division of Children
19 and Family Services of the Department of Health and Human Services, shall
20 have annual training in suicide awareness and prevention. Such training
21 shall include current best practices of behavioral and mental health
22 training with a focus on suicide awareness and prevention prior to any
23 independent contact with youth and families. Such training shall be
24 provided within the framework of existing training programs offered by
25 the department.

14 be considered fully licensed for purposes of federal reimbursement under
15 the federal Fostering Connections to Success and Increasing Adoptions Act

17 (3) The department shall adopt and promulgate rules and regulations
18 establishing new foster home licensing requirements that ensure
19 children's safety, health, and well-being but minimize the use of
20 licensing mandates for nonsafety issues. Such rules and regulations shall
21 provide alternatives to address nonsafety issues regarding housing and
22 provide assistance to families in overcoming licensing barriers,

24 appropriate reimbursement under Title IV-E of the federal Social Security

1 Sec. 4. Section 71-1924, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
2 2024, is amended to read:
3 71-1924 Sections 71-1924 to 71-1951 and section 5 of this act shall
4 be known and may be cited as the Children's Residential Facilities and
5 Placing Licensure Act.
6 Sec. 5. The department shall establish standardized suicide
7 awareness and prevention training requirements for all employees of a
8 child-placing agency prior to any independent contact with youth and
9 families. Such training shall incorporate a baseline of curriculum
10 materials described in section 1 of this act while also allowing a child-
11 placing agency to incorporate training elements that meet the specific
12 needs of the individual agency. The department may approve the existing
13 curriculum of a child-placing agency if such curriculum meets the
14 baseline of curriculum pursuant to section 1 of this act.
15 Sec. 6. This act becomes operative on October 1, 2025.
16 Sec. 7. Original section 71-1904, Reissue Revised Statutes of
17 Nebraska, and section 71-1924, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
18 2024, are repealed.
