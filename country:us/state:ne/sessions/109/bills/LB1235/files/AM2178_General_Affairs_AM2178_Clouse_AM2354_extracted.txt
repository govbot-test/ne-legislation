1 Sec. 4. Section 53-1,100, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is

3 53-1,100 (1) Any person (a) who imports alcoholic liquor for
4 distribution as a wholesaler or distributes or sells alcoholic liquor,
5 other than sale at retail, at any place within the state without having
6 first obtained a valid license to do so under the Nebraska Liquor Control
7 Act, (b) who manufactures alcoholic liquor other than spirits within the
8 state without having first obtained a valid license to do so under the
9 act, (c) who makes any false statement or otherwise violates any of the
10 provisions of the act in obtaining any license under the act, (d) who,
11 having obtained a license under the act, violates any of the provisions
12 of the act with respect to the manufacture, possession, distribution, or
13 sale of alcoholic liquor or with respect to the maintenance of the
14 licensed premises, or (e) who violates any other provision of the act for
15 which a penalty is not otherwise provided, shall for a first offense be
16 guilty of a Class IV misdemeanor and for a second or subsequent offense
17 shall be guilty of a Class II misdemeanor.
18 (2) Any person who (a) manufactures spirits at any place within the
19 state or (b) sells alcoholic liquor at retail without having first
20 obtained a valid license to do so under the act shall be guilty of a
21 Class I misdemeanor for a first offense and a Class IV felony for a
22 second or subsequent offense.
23 (3) Each day any person engages in business as a manufacturer,
24 wholesaler, retailer, or bottle club in violation of the act shall
25 constitute a separate offense.
26 (4) In any prosecution in which a person is charged with an offense
27 arising out of the failure to obtain a valid license as provided in
28 subdivision (1)(a) or (b) or subsection (2) of this section, evidence of
29 the failure of the accused to produce such license upon demand shall
30 constitute prima facie proof that a license has not been issued by the
31 commission to such person.
