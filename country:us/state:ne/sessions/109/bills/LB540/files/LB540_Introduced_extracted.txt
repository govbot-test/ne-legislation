1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to the Nebraska Apiary Act; to amend sections
2 81-2,165.01 and 81-2,166, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to
3 define a term; to create the Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board and
4 provide objectives and duties for such board; to provide a
5 requirement for the Board of Regents of the University of Nebraska
6 to create a full-time educator position as prescribed; to harmonize
7 provisions; and to repeal the original sections.

19 Sec. 3. (1) The Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board is created.
20 (2) The Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board shall consist of the director
21 and the following members appointed by the director:
22 (a) At least one beekeeper who keeps at least one apiary in Nebraska
23 and is also an active member of a beekeeping club or organization;
24 (b) At least one commercial beekeeper who resides in Nebraska; and
25 (c) At least one beekeeper who keeps bees either as a hobbyist, with
26 little to no income generated, or as a sideliner, for whom beekeeping
27 serves as a secondary source of income.
28 (3) The Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board may include up to one
29 representative from the University of Nebraska who specializes in
30 entomology and posses apiculture expertise. Any such representative shall
31 be invited by the director to be a member of the Nebraska Apiary Advisory

3 (4) In the event of a vacancy by an appointed member of the Nebraska

11 Sec. 4. The objectives of the Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board
12 include, but are not limited to:
13 (1) Provide feedback to identify research gaps and prioritize needs
14 to improve the health and habitat of managed and wild bees in Nebraska;
15 (2) Provide feedback to determine actionable steps and protection
16 plans focused on resolving issues identified by the Apiary Advisory
17 Board; and
18 (3) Work with the Legislature to achieve a healthy and sustainable
19 bee population in Nebraska.
20 Sec. 5. The Nebraska Apiary Advisory Board shall examine existing
21 statutes, rules, regulations, policies, and practices in Nebraska and the
22 United States relating to bee populations and make recommendations that
23 include the following:
24 (1) How the state should focus efforts relating to understanding,
25 preventing, and recovering from bee population losses;
26 (2) The food production systems in Nebraska that rely on bee
27 populations and the impact on such systems without sufficient bee
28 populations; and
29 (3) How the state should develop and coordinate education programs
30 to promote public knowledge about the effects of the loss of the bee
31 population and how private individuals and businesses can help address
