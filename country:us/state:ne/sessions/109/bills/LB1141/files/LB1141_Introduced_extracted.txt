1 Section 1. (1) The Legislature finds that:
2 (a) Every Nebraska youth should have the opportunity to reach their
3 full potential, and some youth require additional guidance and support to
4 reach their goals and make positive changes in their lives through job
5 training and apprenticeships;
6 (b) Conserving and developing natural resources and enhancing and
7 maintaining environmentally important land and water through the
8 employment of Nebraska's opportunity youth is beneficial not only to the
9 youth, by providing education and employment opportunities, but also to
10 the state's economy and environment; and
11 (c) The Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation Program will
12 offer Nebraska a unique opportunity to meet the goals of increasing
13 understanding and appreciation of the environment and supporting
14 opportunity youth to reach their full potential.
15 (2) It is the intent of the Legislature:
16 (a) That Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation Program
17 participants complete participation to be exposed to building
18 transferable employment skills;
19 (b) That the program combines academic, environmental, and job
20 skills training with personal growth opportunities to develop youth who
21 can make substantial contributions as Nebraska workers and residents; and
22 (c) To ensure that the program is self-sustaining with stable and
23 predictable funding and, in pursuit of such goal, that all state agencies
24 look to the program first to have its participants engage in projects
25 that further the mission of the program.
26 Sec. 2. For purposes of sections 1 to 6 of this act:
27 (1) Commission means the Game and Parks Commission; and
28 (2) Opportunity youth means a child who (a) has been impacted
29 directly by trauma, including adverse childhood experiences, (b) has had
30 negative contact with law enforcement, or (c) is not experiencing success
31 in school or is in jeopardy of not completing school.

1 Sec. 3. (1) The Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation Program
2 is created. The purpose of the program is to employ Nebraska's
3 opportunity youth on projects which contribute to conserving or
4 developing natural resources and enhancing and maintaining
5 environmentally important land and water under the jurisdiction of the
6 commission and the Department of Labor. The program shall combine
7 academic, environmental, and job skills training with personal growth
8 opportunities for the participants. Beginning January 1, 2027, the
9 commission shall administer and maintain the Nebraska Youth Pre-
10 Apprentice Conservation Program in collaboration with other appropriate
11 state agencies.
12 (2) Participants in the Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation
13 Program shall be opportunity youth who are (a) sixteen years of age or
14 older and younger than twenty-one years of age, (b) unemployed, (c)
15 residents of Nebraska, and (d) on probation or supervision or assigned to
16 a diversion program. The commission shall develop a process for
17 opportunity youth to apply to or be selected for the program. Special
18 effort shall be made to select as participants opportunity youth residing
19 in a qualified census tract as defined in section 81-12,240.
20 (3) Participants in the Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation
21 Program shall be paid not less than the minimum wage. Participation in
22 the program shall be for a period of six weeks for each participant.
23 (4) Participants in the Nebraska Youth Pre-Apprentice Conservation
24 Program shall not be considered state employees for any purpose,
25 including, but not limited to, the State Employees Collective Bargaining
26 Act, the State Employees Retirement Act, the Employment Security Law, the
27 state employees group health insurance program and any other group
28 insurance available to state employees, vacation leave, sick leave, or
29 any other paid leave program available to state employees, any other
30 benefits offered to state employees based on their status as state
31 employees, hours of work, rates of compensation, or leave under the
