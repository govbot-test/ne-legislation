1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to postsecondary education; to amend section
2 85-502, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to change residence
3 requirements relating to tuition and fees at state postsecondary
4 educational institutions; and to repeal the original section.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB1061 LB1061
2026 2026
1 Section 1. Section 85-502, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 85-502 Rules and regulations established by the governing board of
4 each state postsecondary educational institution shall require as a
5 minimum that a person is not deemed to have established a residence in
6 this state, for purposes of sections 85-501 to 85-504, unless:
7 (1) Such person is of legal age or is an emancipated minor and has
8 established a home in Nebraska where he or she is habitually present for
9 a minimum period of one hundred eighty days, with the bona fide intention
10 of making this state his or her permanent residence, supported by
11 documentary proof;
12 (2) The parents, parent, or guardian having custody of a minor
13 registering in the educational institution have established a home in
14 Nebraska where such parents, parent, or guardian are or is habitually
15 present with the bona fide intention to make this state their, his, or
16 her permanent residence, supported by documentary proof. If a student has
17 matriculated in any state postsecondary educational institution while his
18 or her parents, parent, or guardian had an established home in this
19 state, and the parents, parent, or guardian ceases to reside in the
20 state, such student shall not thereby lose his or her resident status if
21 such student has the bona fide intention to make this state his or her
22 permanent residence, supported by documentary proof;
23 (3) Such student is of legal age and is a dependent for federal
24 income tax purposes of a parent or former guardian who has established a
25 home in Nebraska where he or she is habitually present with the bona fide
26 intention of making this state his or her permanent residence, supported
27 by documentary proof;
28 (4) Such student is a nonresident of this state prior to marriage
29 and marries a person who has established a home in Nebraska where he or
30 she is habitually present with the bona fide intention of making this
31 state his or her permanent residence, supported by documentary proof;
-2-

1 (5) Such Except as provided in subdivision (9) of this section, such
2 student, if an alien, has applied to or has a petition pending with the
3 United States Immigration and Naturalization Service to attain lawful
4 status under federal immigration law and has established a home in
5 Nebraska for a period of at least one hundred eighty days where he or she
6 is habitually present with the bona fide intention to become a lawful
7 permanent resident of the United States and make this state his or her
8 permanent residence, supported by documentary proof;
9 (6) Such student is a staff member or a dependent of a staff member
10 of the University of Nebraska, one of the Nebraska state colleges, or one
11 of the community college areas who joins the staff immediately prior to
12 the beginning of a term from an out-of-state location;
13 (7)(a) Such student is on active duty with the armed services of the
14 United States and has been assigned a permanent duty station in Nebraska;
15 or
16 (b) Such student is a spouse or legal dependent of a person who was
17 on active duty with the armed services of the United States assigned to a
18 permanent duty station in Nebraska at the time such student was accepted
19 for admission to the state postsecondary educational institution and such
20 student remains continually enrolled at such state postsecondary
21 educational institution;
22 (8) Such student is currently serving in the Nebraska National
23 Guard; or
24 (9)(a) Such student resided with his or her parent, guardian, or
25 conservator while attending a public, private, denominational, or
26 parochial high school in this state or a school in this state which
27 elects pursuant to section 79-1601 not to meet accreditation or approval
28 requirements and:
29 (i) Graduated from a public, private, denominational, or parochial
30 high school in this state, completed the program of instruction offered
31 by a school in this state which elects pursuant to section 79-1601 not to
-3-
LB1061 LB1061
2026 2026
1 meet accreditation or approval requirements, or received a diploma of
2 high school equivalency issued pursuant to section 79-730;
3 (ii) Resided in this state for at least three years before the date
4 the student graduated from the high school, completed the program of
5 instruction, or received the diploma of high school equivalency;
6 (iii) Registered as an entering student in a state postsecondary
7 educational institution not earlier than the 2006 fall semester; and
8 (iv) Provided to the state postsecondary educational institution an
9 affidavit stating that he or she will file an application to become a
10 permanent resident at the earliest opportunity he or she is eligible to
11 do so.
12 (b) If the parent, guardian, or conservator with whom the student
13 resided ceases to reside in the state, such student shall not lose his or
14 her resident status under this subdivision if the student has the bona
15 fide intention to make this state his or her permanent residence,
16 supported by documentary proof; or
17 (9) (10) Such student is qualified for a national service
18 educational award or summer of service educational award from the
19 National Service Trust pursuant to 42 U.S.C. 12602, as such section
20 existed on January 1, 2021.
21 Sec. 2. Original section 85-502, Reissue Revised Statutes of
22 Nebraska, is repealed.
