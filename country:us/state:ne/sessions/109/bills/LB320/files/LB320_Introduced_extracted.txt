1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to human trafficking; to amend sections
2 81-1430 and 81-1431, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to change
3 provisions relating to the human trafficking task force; to require
4 hotels and similar public lodging establishments place posters
5 relating to human trafficking in such establishments; to provide for
6 training on human trafficking to employees and similar public
7 lodging establishments; to provide powers and duties to the
8 Department of Labor; to limit the liability of owners, operators,
9 and employees of a hotel or similar public lodging establishment
10 relating to human trafficking committed by a third party; to
11 harmonize provisions; and to repeal the original sections.
12 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

3 81-1430 (1) A task force is hereby established within the Nebraska
4 Commission on Law Enforcement and Criminal Justice for the purposes of
5 investigating and studying human trafficking, the methods for advertising
6 human trafficking services, and the victimization of individuals coerced
7 to participate in human trafficking.
8 (2) The task force shall examine the extent to which human
9 trafficking is prevalent in this state, the scope of efforts being taken
10 to prevent human trafficking from occurring, and the services available
11 to victims of human trafficking in this state. The task force shall
12 utilize information and research available from the Innocence Lost
13 National Initiative. The task force shall research and recommend a model
14 of rehabilitative services for victims of human trafficking that includes
15 input from the areas of law enforcement, social services, the legal
16 profession, the judiciary, mental health, and immigration. The task force
17 shall also investigate the limitations upon victims who wish to come
18 forward and seek medical attention; investigate the potential to stop
19 human trafficking; and investigate the potential to promote recovery, to
20 protect families and children who may be profoundly impacted by such
21 abuse, and to save lives.
22 (3)(a) The Department of Labor shall work with the task force to
23 develop or select informational posters for placement around the state.
24 The posters shall be in English, Spanish, and any other language deemed
25 appropriate by the task force. The posters shall include a toll-free
26 telephone number a person may call for assistance, preferably the
27 National Human Trafficking Resource Center Hotline (888)373-7888.
28 (b) Posters shall be placed in rest stops, strip clubs, and casinos,
29 and hotels and similar public lodging establishments. The task force

6 Sec. 3. (1) For purposes of this section:
7 (a) Hotel means a facility in which the public may, for a
8 consideration, obtain sleeping accommodations. Hotel includes hotels,
9 motels, tourist homes, campgrounds, courts, lodging houses, inns, state-
10 operated hotels, and nonprofit hotels but does not include hospitals,
11 sanitariums, nursing homes, long-term care facilities, or dormitories or
12 facilities operated by an educational institution and regularly used to
13 house students; and
14 (b) Human trafficking means labor trafficking, labor trafficking of
15 a minor, sex trafficking, or sex trafficking of a minor as those terms
16 are defined in section 28-830.
17 (2) Any person, firm, or corporation that owns or operates a hotel
18 or similar public lodging establishment may:
19 (a) Provide to all employees of such hotel or public lodging
20 establishment within the State of Nebraska training on human trafficking
21 that has been approved pursuant to subsection (2) of section 81-1431
22 within one hundred eighty days of being employed at such hotel or public
23 lodging establishment;
24 (b) Implement procedures for the reporting of suspected human
25 trafficking to the National Human Trafficking Resource Center Hotline or
26 to a local enforcement agency; and
27 (c) Implement a human trafficking prevention policy for employees of
28 the hotel or establishment within the State of Nebraska.
29 (3) An owner, operator, or employee of a hotel or similar public
30 lodging establishment who implements the training and policies as
31 prescribed in subsection (2) of this section shall not be liable for any
