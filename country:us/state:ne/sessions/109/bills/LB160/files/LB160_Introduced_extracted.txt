1 Section 1. Section 71-216.01, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska,
2 is amended to read:
3 71-216.01 An applicant who fails to pass the barber examination may
4 take the examination a second time when the examination is offered by the
5 board without being required to take any further course of study. If an
6 applicant fails the examination a second time, the applicant shall be
7 required to complete a further course of study in barbering, consisting
8 of five hundred hours with not more than ten hours completed in any one
9 working day in a school of barbering. An applicant may have an
10 opportunity to test out of the course of study after completion of two
11 hundred fifty hours with the approval of the board. If such applicant
12 fails to test out of the examination after completion of two hundred
13 fifty hours, the applicant shall complete the remaining two hundred fifty
14 hours before such applicant shall be permitted to take the examination a
15 third time.
16 A graduate from a school of barbering who fails to pass a
17 satisfactory examination may take the examination next time that the
18 examination is given by the Board of Barber Examiners without being
19 required to take any further course of study. Should the applicant fail
20 the examination a second time, the applicant shall be required to
21 complete a further course of study of not less than five hundred hours to
22 be completed within three months of not more than ten hours in any one
23 working day in a school of barbering approved by the Board of Barber
24 Examiners before the applicant may be permitted to take the examination a
25 third time.
26 Sec. 2. Original section 71-216.01, Reissue Revised Statutes of
27 Nebraska, is repealed.
