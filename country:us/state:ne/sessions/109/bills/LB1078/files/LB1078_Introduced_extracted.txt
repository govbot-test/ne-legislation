1 Section 1. Sections 1 to 5 of this act shall be known and may be
2 cited as the Fair Online Pricing Act.
3 Sec. 2. For purposes of the Fair Online Pricing Act:
4 (1) Coupon means any method by which a consumer receives a discount
5 on the purchase of any item that is funded, produced, sponsored,
6 promoted, or furnished, either directly or indirectly, by a business,
7 including any paper coupon, digital coupon, or instant redeemable coupon;
8 (2) Discount means any reduction from the regular or advertised
9 price offered to consumers on a temporary or conditional basis;
10 (3) Hardware state means a condition or mode of existence of a
11 system, component, or simulation, including the battery life of, number
12 of wireless connections detected by, hardware performance characteristics
13 of, and age of the device, and data that is erased or retained when an
14 online device is restarted or powered down; and
15 (4) Online device means a physical object that has built-in
16 resources that allow it to communicate through the Internet or a short-
17 range wireless technology and react to interface conditions, including
18 any laptop computer, desktop computer, tablet, smartphone, or other smart
19 hardware.
20 Sec. 3. (1) A price offered to a consumer through the consumer's
21 online device shall not be generated in whole, or in part, based on any
22 of the following input data:
23 (a) The hardware or hardware state of the online device. This
24 subdivision (a) does not prohibit the generation of a price offered to a
25 consumer based on the hardware or hardware state of the online device for
26 the following purposes:
27 (i) Any repair or maintenance of the online device; or
28 (ii) Calculating a trade-in value of the online device;
29 (b) The presence or absence of any software on the online device; or
30 (c) Geolocation data of the online device, when used to set a price
31 based on inferences about the consumer made from such data. This

1 subdivision (c) does not prohibit the use of geolocation data of the
2 online device and the consumer's location to generate a price for any

4 (i) To determine pricing based on the real-time demand for the

7 (ii) To provide accurate pricing information based on price

12 (2) The Fair Online Pricing Act does not prohibit any coupon;
13 discount; or other bona fide rebate, sale, or promotion that:
14 (a) Is separate from the price of any good or service;
15 (b) Reduces the price of any good or service;
16 (c) Is issued or offered to the general public on the same terms;
17 and
18 (d) Does not incorporate input data as specified in subsection (1)
19 of this section.
20 Sec. 4. (1) A person who violates the Fair Online Pricing Act is
21 liable for a civil penalty in an amount not to exceed ten thousand
22 dollars for each violation.
23 (2) The Attorney General may bring an action in the name of the
24 State of Nebraska to:
25 (a) Recover a civil penalty under this section;
26 (b) Restrain or enjoin the person from violating the Fair Online
27 Pricing Act; or
28 (c) Recover the civil penalty and seek injunctive relief.
29 (3) The Attorney General may recover reasonable attorney's fees and
30 other reasonable expenses incurred in investigating and bringing an
31 action under this section.
