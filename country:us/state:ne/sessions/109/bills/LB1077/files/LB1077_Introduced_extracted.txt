2 is amended to read:
3 48-146.03 (1) Each workers' compensation insurance policy issued by
4 an insurer pursuant to the Nebraska Workers' Compensation Act:
5 (a) Shall offer, at the option of the insured employer, a deductible
6 for medical benefits in the amount of five hundred dollars to two
7 thousand five hundred dollars per claim in increments of five hundred
8 dollars; or
9 (b) May offer, at the option of the insured employer and the
10 workers' compensation insurer, a deductible for all amounts paid by the
11 insurer as long as the deductible is not more than forty percent of the
12 insured employer's otherwise applicable annual workers' compensation
13 insurance premium at rates approved for the insurer but not less than
14 fifty thousand dollars.
15 The insured employer, if choosing to exercise one of such options
16 listed in this subsection, may choose only one of the amounts as the
17 deductible. The provisions of this section shall be fully disclosed to
18 each prospective purchaser in writing.
19 (2) The deductible form shall provide that the workers' compensation
20 insurer shall remain liable for and shall pay the entire cost of medical
21 benefits for each claim directly to the medical provider, shall remain
22 liable for and pay the entire cost of benefits, claims, and expenses as
23 required by the policy irrespective of the deductible provision, and
24 shall then be reimbursed by the employer for any deductible amounts paid
25 by the workers' compensation insurer. The employer shall be liable for
26 reimbursement up to the limit of the deductible.
27 (3) Beginning January 1, 2027, losses paid by the employer under the
28 deductible shall be credited against the employer's experience
29 modification while the deductible option is used, unless the employer
30 elects to purchase a gross reportable deductible policy.
31 (4) (3) A workers' compensation insurer shall not be required to
