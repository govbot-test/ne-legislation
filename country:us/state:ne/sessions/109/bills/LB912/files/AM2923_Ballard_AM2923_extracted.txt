(Amendments to E&R amendments, ER144)
Introduced by Ballard, 21.
1 1. Insert the following new sections:
3 2024, is amended to read:
4 38-2101 Sections 38-2101 to 38-2147 and section 14 of this act shall
5 be known and may be cited as the Mental Health Practice Act.
6 Sec. 14. (1) Every two years, a minimum of two hours of continuing
7 education in domestic abuse counseling shall be required for all licensed
8 mental health practitioners and licensed independent mental health
9 practitioners under the Mental Health Practice Act.
10 (2) Such training shall be conducted by an individual who has (a)
11 received the domestic abuse training required in section 38-2123, (b) a
12 minimum of three years' experience working with victims of domestic abuse
13 or offenders, and (c) completed an additional forty hours in domestic
14 abuse-related training.
15 (3) For purposes of the section, domestic abuse means abuse as
16 defined in section 42-903.
17 Sec. 15. Section 38-2123, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
18 2024, is amended to read:
19 38-2123 (1) A person who needs to obtain the required three thousand
20 hours of supervised experience in mental health practice as specified in
21 section 38-2122 to qualify for a mental health practitioner license shall
22 obtain a provisional mental health practitioner license. To qualify for a
23 provisional mental health practitioner license, such person shall:
24 (a) Have a master's degree, a doctoral degree, or the equivalent of
25 a master's degree, as determined by the board, that consists of course
26 work and training which was primarily therapeutic mental health in

1 content and included a practicum or internship and was from a mental
2 health program as specified in section 38-2122;
3 (b) Apply prior to earning the three thousand hours of supervised
4 experience; and
5 (c) Pay the provisional mental health practitioner license fee;
6 and .
7 (d) Beginning September 1, 2027, obtain three hours of training
8 relating to domestic abuse, which shall include: (i) Appropriate
9 screening tools for victims of domestic abuse; (ii) indicators of high
10 lethality or risk; (iii) documentation standards; (iv) appropriate
11 referrals to domestic abuse specific services, advocacy, and systems
12 navigation professionals; (v) impact on children; (vi) ethical practice
13 and boundaries; and (vii) trauma-informed, victim-centered care and
14 safety planning. For purposes of this subdivision (1)(d), domestic abuse
15 means abuse as defined in section 42-903.
16 (2) An individual or organization providing the training described
17 in subdivision (1)(d) of this section shall submit all training materials
18 to the board for approval.
19 (3) (2) The rules and regulations approved by the board and adopted
20 and promulgated by the department shall not require that the applicant
21 have a supervisor in place at the time of application for a provisional
22 mental health practitioner license.
23 (4) (3) A provisional mental health practitioner license shall
24 expire upon receipt of licensure as a mental health practitioner or five
25 years after the date of issuance, whichever comes first.
26 (5) (4) A person who holds a provisional mental health practitioner
27 license shall inform all clients that he or she holds a provisional
28 license and is practicing mental health under supervision and shall
29 identify the supervisor. Failure to make such disclosure is a ground for
30 discipline as set forth in section 38-2139.
