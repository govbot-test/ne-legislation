1 Section 1. The Nebraska State Patrol shall conduct a study to
2 determine how to increase state criminal justice protective and
3 investigative resources for reporting and identifying missing Black women
4 and children in Nebraska. The Nebraska State Patrol shall work with the
5 Commission on African American Affairs to convene meetings with state and
6 local law enforcement partners, federal agencies, and urban community
7 organizations to determine the scope of the problem, identify barriers,
8 and find ways to create partnerships to increase reporting and
9 investigation of missing Black women and children. The Nebraska State
10 Patrol shall work with the United States Department of Justice to
11 increase information sharing and resource coordination to focus on
12 reporting and investigating missing Black women and children in Nebraska.
13 The Nebraska State Patrol shall submit a report electronically to the
14 Executive Board of the Legislative Council by June 1, 2027, on the
15 results of such study. Such report shall include data and analysis of the
16 number of missing Black women and children in Nebraska, identification of
17 barriers in providing state resources to address the issue, and
18 recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to improve the
19 reporting and identification of missing Black women and children in
20 Nebraska.
