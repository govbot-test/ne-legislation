1 Section 1. Section 38-2850, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 38-2850 As authorized by the Uniform Credentialing Act, the practice
4 of pharmacy may be engaged in by a pharmacist, a pharmacist intern, or a
5 practitioner with a pharmacy license. The practice of pharmacy shall not
6 be construed to include any of the following:
7 (1) Practitioners, other than veterinarians, certified nurse
8 midwives, certified registered nurse anesthetists, nurse practitioners,
9 and physician assistants, who dispense drugs or devices as an incident to
10 the practice of their profession, except that if such practitioner
11 engages in dispensing such drugs or devices to his or her patients for
12 which such patients are charged, such practitioner shall obtain a
13 pharmacy license;
14 (2) Persons who sell, offer, or expose for sale nonprescription
15 drugs or proprietary medicines, the sale of which is not in itself a
16 violation of the Nebraska Liquor Control Act;
17 (3) Medical representatives, detail persons, or persons known by
18 some name of like import, but only to the extent of permitting the
19 relating of pharmaceutical information to health care professionals;
20 (4) Licensed veterinarians practicing within the scope of their
21 profession;
22 (5) Certified nurse midwives, certified registered nurse
23 anesthetists, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants who dispense
24 sample medications which are provided by the manufacturer and are
25 dispensed at no charge to the patient;
26 (6) Optometrists who prescribe or dispense eyeglasses or contact
27 lenses to their own patients, including contact lenses that contain and
28 deliver ocular pharmaceutical agents as authorized under the Optometry
