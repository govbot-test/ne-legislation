2026 2026
LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA
ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
SECOND SESSION
Introduced by Hardin, 48.
Read first time January 09, 2026
Committee: Health and Human Services
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to the Alzheimer's Disease and Other Dementia
2 Support Act; to amend section 71-561, Revised Statutes Cumulative
3 Supplement, 2024; to require the Department of Health and Human
4 Services to appoint a dementia services coordinator as prescribed;
5 and to repeal the original section.
6 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB913 LB913
2026 2026
1 Section 1. Section 71-561, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
2 2024, is amended to read:
3 71-561 Sections 71-561 to 71-567 and section 2 of this act shall be
4 known and may be cited as the Alzheimer's Disease and Other Dementia
5 Support Act.
6 Sec. 2. No later than XXX, the department shall appoint a dementia
7 services coordinator who shall:
8 (1) Serve as a referral point for linking families caring for
9 individuals with Alzheimer's disease and other dementia with supportive
10 services and resources;
11 (2) Provide information, counseling, education, and referral about
12 services and programs, including safe, secure environments that support
13 individuals and families dealing with Alzheimer's disease and other
14 dementia;
15 (3) Collect and monitor data related to the impact of Alzheimer's
16 disease and other dementia on residents of this state;
17 (4) Evaluate the needs of individuals with Alzheimer's disease and
18 other dementia and their caregivers and identify the services, resources,
19 and policies required to address such needs;
20 (5) Recommend strategies for coordination of services and resources
21 among agencies involved in the delivery of services to individuals with
22 Alzheimer's disease and other dementia;
23 (6) Monitor and assist development and implementation of the state
24 plan for meeting the needs of individuals with Alzheimer's disease and
25 other dementia and their caregivers;
26 (7) Recommend policies, legislation, and funding necessary to
27 implement the state plan for meeting the needs of individuals with
28 Alzheimer's disease and other dementia and their caregivers;
29 (8) Increase awareness and create dementia-specific training to
30 facilitate access to quality coordinated care for individuals with
31 Alzheimer's disease and other dementia in the most integrated setting;
-2-
LB913 LB913
2026 2026
1 and
2 (9) Organize community stakeholders and resources to identify
3 proactive and effective solutions.
4 Sec. 3. Original section 71-561, Revised Statutes Cumulative
5 Supplement, 2024, is repealed.
-3-
