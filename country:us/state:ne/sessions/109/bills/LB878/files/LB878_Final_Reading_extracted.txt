1 Section 1. (1) For purposes of this section:
2 (a) Agency head means the administrative head of a state agency,
3 such as an agency director, executive director, commissioner, or
4 executive secretary, who reports directly to the Governor, a commission,
5 or a board;
6 (b) Parental leave means leave hours that the state provides to a
7 parental leave eligible employee to bond with a child;
8 (c) Parental leave eligible employee means an individual who:
9 (i) Is a permanent state employee;
10 (ii) Has been employed by the State of Nebraska for at least twelve
11 months and has worked for the State of Nebraska for at least one thousand
12 two hundred fifty hours in the last twelve months or has had such
13 requirements waived by the agency head;
14 (iii) Gives birth to a child or adopts a child who is twelve months
15 of age or younger; and
16 (iv) Is assuming a parental role with respect to the child;
17 (d) Permanent state employee means any state employee in a position
18 worked on an ongoing and continuous basis;
19 (e) Retaliatory action means to do any of the following to an
20 employee:
21 (i) Dismiss the employee;
22 (ii) Reduce the employee's compensation;
23 (iii) Fail to increase the employee's compensation by an amount that
24 the employee is otherwise entitled to or was promised;
25 (iv) Fail to promote the employee if the employee would have
26 otherwise been promoted; or
27 (v) Threaten to take any of the actions described in subdivisions
28 (1)(e)(i) through (iv) of this section; and
29 (f) State employee means any individual who is a full-time employee
30 or a part-time employee of the State of Nebraska.
31 (2) A parental leave eligible employee who is a full-time employee

1 shall be entitled to two hundred forty hours of paid parental leave for
2 the birth or adoption of such employee's child. A parental leave eligible
3 employee who is a part-time employee shall be entitled to paid parental
4 leave proportionate to the employee's regular workweek.
5 (3) Parental leave:
6 (a) Shall not be used before the day on which the parental leave
7 eligible employee's child is born or adopted;
8 (b) Shall not be used more than six months after the day on which
9 the parental leave eligible employee's child is born or adopted;
10 (c) Shall not be used intermittently, unless:
11 (i) By mutual written agreement between the state and the parental
12 leave eligible employee; or
13 (ii) A health care provider certifies that intermittent leave is
14 medically necessary due to a serious health condition of the child; and
15 (d) Runs concurrently with any leave authorized under the Family and
16 Medical Leave Act of 1993, 29 U.S.C. 2601 et seq.
17 (4) A parental leave eligible employee shall not use more than two
18 hundred forty hours of paid parental leave within a single twelve-month
19 period.
20 (5)(a) Except as provided in subdivision (5)(b) of this section, a
21 parental leave eligible employee shall give the state notice at least
22 thirty days before the day on which the employee plans to begin using
23 parental leave under this section.
24 (b) If circumstances beyond the parental leave eligible employee's
25 control prevent the employee from giving notice in accordance with
26 subdivision (5)(a) of this section, the employee shall give such notice
27 as soon as reasonably practicable.
28 (6) Except as provided in subdivision (3)(d) of this section, the
29 state shall not charge parental leave under this section against sick,
30 vacation, compensatory, or other leave a parental leave eligible employee
31 is entitled to.
