1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to civil actions; to amend sections 13-903 and
2 81-8,210, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to provide for a
3 cause of action for deprivation of constitutional rights by law
4 enforcement officers; to define and redefine terms; to eliminate the
5 defense of qualified immunity as prescribed; to require
6 indemnification by public employers; to waive sovereign immunity for
7 certain claims against law enforcement officers and exempt such
8 claims from the Political Subdivisions Tort Claims Act and State
9 Tort Claims Act; to provide an operative date; to provide
10 severability; and to repeal the original sections.
11 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

1 Section 1. The Legislature finds and declares that:
2 (1) The protection of constitutional rights is essential to
3 maintaining public trust in law enforcement;
4 (2) The judicial doctrine of qualified immunity has, in practice,
5 denied many Nebraskans a remedy for violations of their state and federal
6 constitutional rights;
7 (3) The State of Nebraska has a compelling interest in ensuring that
8 all persons within its jurisdiction have access to justice and
9 accountability when their rights are violated by government actors; and
10 (4) It is the intent of the Legislature to create an independent,
11 state-based civil remedy for deprivation of constitutional rights and to
12 eliminate qualified immunity as a defense to such claims.
13 Sec. 2. For purposes of sections 1 to 6 of this act:
14 (1) Constitutional right means any right, privilege, or immunity
15 guaranteed by the Constitution of the United States or the Constitution
16 of Nebraska;
17 (2) Law enforcement officer means:
18 (a) A law enforcement officer as defined in section 81-1401;
19 (b) A peace officer as defined in section 49-801;
20 (c) A correctional officer; or
21 (d) A probation officer;
22 (3)(a) Public employer means the state, any state agency, or any
23 political subdivision.
24 (b) Public employer does not include a contractor of the state, a
25 state agency, or a political subdivision;
26 (4) Political subdivision has the same meaning as in section 13-903;
27 and
28 (5) State agency has the same meaning as in section 81-8,210.
29 Sec. 3. (1) A person who has been deprived of any constitutional
30 right by a law enforcement officer acting under color of law may bring a
31 civil action for appropriate relief.

1 (2) In an action under this section, appropriate relief includes:
2 (a) Compensatory damages, including for physical injury, pain and
3 suffering, emotional distress, loss of liberty, or loss of reputation;
4 (b) Punitive damages when the officer's conduct is determined to be
5 willful, wanton, or malicious;
6 (c) Such preliminary and other equitable or declaratory relief as
7 may be appropriate; and
8 (d) Reasonable attorney's fees and other litigation costs reasonably
9 incurred.
10 (3) In any action brought under this section, qualified immunity or
11 any substantially similar defense shall not be available to the
12 defendant.
13 (4) Any punitive damages awarded under this section shall be
14 remitted to the State Treasurer for distribution in accordance with
15 Article VII, section 5, of the Constitution of Nebraska.
16 (5) The cause of action provided for in this section is independent
17 of and in addition to any other remedy provided by state or federal law.
18 Sec. 4. (1) A public employer shall indemnify its law enforcement
19 officers for any judgment entered against them in an action under section
20 3 of this act, unless the officer acted in bad faith or with malicious
21 intent.
22 (2) If the public employer determines that indemnification is not
23 required under subsection (1) of this section, it shall provide written
24 notice to the officer and the plaintiff within thirty days after the
25 entry of judgment.
26 Sec. 5. An action brought under section 3 of this act shall be
27 filed within three years after the cause of action accrues.
28 Sec. 6. (1) Sovereign immunity is waived for any claim brought
29 pursuant to section 3 of this act.
30 (2) A claim under section 3 of this act is not subject to the
31 Political Subdivisions Tort Claims Act or the State Tort Claims Act.
