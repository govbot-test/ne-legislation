9 Sec. 4. (1) The Legislature finds that: (a) Doula services have
10 been proven to reduce the cost of birthing and improve outcomes for
11 mothers and infants; (b) one of the most effective services to improve
12 labor and delivery outcomes is the continuous presence of support
13 personnel such as a doula; and (c) support from a doula is associated
14 with lower cesarean rates, as well as fewer obstetric interventions,
15 fewer complications, less pain medication, shorter labor hours, and
16 higher Apgar scores for infants.
17 (2) No later than January 1, 2029, the department shall reimburse a
18 provider for doula services. Such reimbursement shall be paid by state
19 funds at rates determined by the department. The department shall submit
20 a state plan amendment, if necessary, to provide for reimbursement of
21 doula services.
22 (3)(a) The department shall establish a work group of stakeholders
23 and experts to develop an implementation plan, including appropriate
24 reimbursement rates and appropriate training, certification, or
25 experience requirements for doula services. The work group shall submit
26 the implementation plan to the department no later than January 1, 2027.
27 (b) The work group shall be comprised of the following: (i) Thirty
28 percent of the members shall represent the doula profession; (ii) thirty
29 percent of the members shall represent communities of color
30 disproportionately impacted by poor birth outcomes; (iii) ten percent of
31 the members shall represent rural Nebraska; and (iv) ten percent of the
-8-

LB958 LB958
2026 2026
1 members shall have utilized doula services.
2 (c) Additional members of the work group shall include, but not be
3 limited to: (i) Medical providers; (ii) public health professionals;
4 (iii) representatives of tribal organizations; and (iv) community
5 advocates.
6 (4)(a) For purposes of this section, doula means a trained
7 professional who provides emotional, physical, and informational support
8 for individuals before, during, and after labor and birth. This includes,
9 but is not limited to, attending prenatal visits, support during
10 delivery, and providing resources during the postpartum period.
11 (b) A doula shall have appropriate training, certification, or
12 experience, as determined by the implementation plan developed by the
13 work group described in subdivision (3)(a) of this section.
14 (c) A doula shall not perform clinical or medical tasks and shall
15 not diagnose or treat in any modality.
16 (5) It is the intent of the Legislature to fund the state portion of
17 reimbursement for doula services from the vital statistics subfund of the
18 Health and Human Services Cash Fund.
